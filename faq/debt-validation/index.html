What is debt validation?

Debt validation is a formal request for a collector to prove the debt is accurate and that they have the right to collect it. It helps you confirm balances, ownership, and the correct creditor.

Confirm the original creditor: Know who first issued the account.
Verify the balance: Ensure the amount includes accurate fees and interest.
Check ownership: Confirm whether the collector owns the debt or is collecting for the creditor.

When should you request it?

The best time is right after you receive the first written notice. Make the request in writing and keep a copy for your records.

Timeline tip: You typically have 30 days from the initial notice to request validation. Sending the request quickly can pause collection efforts until verification arrives.

Always save copies, send letters by certified mail when possible, and track delivery.

What to include in your validation request

Ask for the details that prove the collector has the right information and the right to collect.

Current balance breakdown

Request principal, interest, and fees so you can verify the total amount claimed.

Original creditor details

Confirm the name of the original creditor and the original account number.

Ownership documentation

Ask for proof of assignment or purchase if the collector says they own the debt.
