Struggling with Credit Card Debt?

You’re not alone. The average household owes over $9,000 — and rising interest rates make it worse. Here's what you can do now.

The Trap: Minimum Payments

Paying the minimum on a $10,000 balance with 22% interest could take 20+ years — and cost more than $24,000. That’s not relief — that’s a trap.

Real Relief Options

  • Debt Settlement: Negotiate to pay less than you owe — usually 40–60% of total.
  • Consolidation Loans: Lower interest rates if your credit still qualifies.
  • Balance Transfers: Good for short-term relief but risky if not paid off fast.
  • Hardship Plans: Some creditors offer temporary relief — but you have to ask.

Avoid Common Pitfalls

  • Beware of “instant forgiveness” scams
  • Never pay upfront fees for debt help
  • Don’t ignore the problem — missed payments snowball fast
