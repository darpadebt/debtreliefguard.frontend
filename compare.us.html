Who typically qualifies

We recommend comparing at least two options before deciding.

Debt Settlement

Negotiate balances with creditors for less than owed.

Usually lower than minimums; varies by hardship & balances.
~24–48 months typical.
May decrease during program; can recover post‑completion.
No upfront fees; success‑based fees only when settlements are reached.*
Hardship or behind on payments; unsecured debts only.

Consolidation Loan

One loan to pay off many balances.

Payment depends on rate & term; often near current minimums.
36–60+ months typical.
Requires credit check; can help if rate is much lower.
Origination fees may apply; interest accrues.
Best for steady income & fair‑to‑good credit.

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

Agency consolidates payments; interest concessions only.

Often close to current minimums (reduced APR helps).
~36–60 months typical.
Usually neutral after enrollment; requires on‑time payments.
Monthly admin fees may apply; no balance reduction.
Current or slightly behind; steady income needed.

Bankruptcy

Federal court process to discharge or restructure debts.

Payment depends on Chapter (7 vs 13) and court plan.
Several months (Ch.7) or 36–60 months (Ch.13).
Major, long‑term credit impact; may be best for severe hardship.
Court & attorney fees apply.
Best discussed with a licensed attorney.

*Fees are collected only after a settlement agreement is reached and a payment is made, consistent with FTC rules.
