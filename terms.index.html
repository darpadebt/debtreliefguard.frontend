What counts as legitimate debt relief? (Educational)

Legitimate options include debt consolidation loans, debt settlement, and credit counseling. Trusted providers follow the FTC’s Telemarketing Sales Rule (no upfront fees for settlement), offer clear disclosures, and help compare payment ranges and timelines. We prioritize FTC‑aligned guidance and scam‑prevention tips so you can choose confidently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does debt relief hurt my credit?

It depends on the path you choose. Debt consolidation loans may trigger a hard inquiry, which can cause a small, temporary dip. Debt settlement usually has a more significant short‑term impact as accounts may become delinquent during negotiations, but it can reduce your total payoff. Credit counseling typically avoids hard inquiries and focuses on budgeting and interest concessions.

Is debt settlement legitimate?

Yes—when handled by providers who follow the FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule: no upfront fees for settlement services, clear disclosures, and written terms. Legitimate programs set expectations around timelines, impact, and fees before you enroll.

Are debt consolidation loans a good idea?

They can be if you qualify for a lower rate and can maintain on‑time payments. A consolidation loan may reduce interest and simplify to one payment.
