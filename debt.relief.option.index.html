Compare Your Debt Relief Options

Not all debt relief is the same. Let’s break down your main paths — including what’s safe, what’s risky, and what works.

💸 Debt Settlement

Negotiate with creditors to reduce what you owe. Typically 30–60% reduction.

  • Best for: Large unsecured debt ($10k+)
  • Risks: May impact credit short-term
  • FTC warning: No upfront fees allowed

💳 Debt Consolidation

Combine multiple debts into one loan with a lower interest rate.

  • Best for: Good credit + stable income
  • Pros: Lower monthly payments
  • Cons: Loan required (approval not guaranteed)

⚖️ Bankruptcy

Last resort legal option to discharge debt — often for medical, legal, or total default.

  • Chapter 7: Full discharge (must qualify)
  • Chapter 13: Court-approved repayment
  • May stay on credit for 7–10 years

📊 Debt Management Plan

Work with a nonprofit or agency to pay off debt over time with lower interest.

  • Best for: Moderate credit card debt
  • Usually involves monthly budget planning
  • Some plans are free; others charge a fee
