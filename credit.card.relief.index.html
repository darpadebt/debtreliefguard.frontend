Why minimum payments don’t work

The trap: On a $10,000 balance at 22% APR, minimum payments can stretch 20+ years and cost >$24,000. Relief means changing the math—not just paying more minimums.

Real relief options

  • Debt Settlement: Resolve accounts for less than you owe—often 40–60% of balances.
  • Consolidation Loan: Replace multiple cards with one lower-rate payment (credit/income required).
  • Balance Transfer: 0% promo periods can help if you can aggressively pay down principal.
  • Hardship Plans: Ask issuers for temporary interest/fee relief if you’ve had a qualifying hardship.

Avoid common pitfalls

  • Beware “instant forgiveness” or “erase debt” scams.
  • Never pay upfront fees for debt relief—FTC rules prohibit them.
  • Don’t delay—late fees and penalty APRs snowball fast.
