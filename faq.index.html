Debt Relief Red Flags

If you are comparing debt relief providers, a quick verification checklist can help you stay in control and avoid preventable risks.

  • Upfront fees before any results or services are delivered.
  • Guaranteed outcomes or guaranteed savings promises.
  • High-pressure tactics like ‚Äúact today‚Äù language or refusing to give you time to review.
  • Vague terms or refusal to provide disclosures in writing.
  • Requests for bank login credentials, remote-access apps, or screen sharing into your banking.
  • Claims that a hard credit pull is required just to see options.
  • Refusing to provide business identity details such as address, phone number, written agreement, or cancellation policy.
  • Advising you to stop paying creditors without clearly explaining potential consequences and alternatives.

What to do instead

  • Get terms and fees in writing.
  • Ask when and under what conditions any fee is earned or charged.
  • Verify the company name, address, and how to contact support.
  • Confirm privacy expectations and what information will and will not be requested.
  • Keep records of communications and documents.
